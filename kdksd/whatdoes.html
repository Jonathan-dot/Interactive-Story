What Does It Do?

Augmented Reality is very similar to virtual reality but works with mostly phone apps.

AR shows items as if they were in the real world, even though they are just produced inside of a singular app.

An example dsdsdsof Augmented Reality’s in app power, “If you experienced the hubbub of Pokemon Go, you witnessed augmented reality in action. This (once incredibly popular) mobile game allowed users to view the world around them through their smartphone cameras while projecting game items, including onscreen icons, score, and ever-elusive Pokemon creatures, as overlays that made them seem as if those items were right in your real-life neighborhood.” (How Augmented Reality Works)

The Negatives to Augmented Reality...

A website called ‘Forbes’, talks about how there might be privacy risks to AR due to the facial expressions it sees on one’s and the data AR will collect from your daily life.

According to Forbes “Highly Popular AR apps of the type power the filters used by Snapchat and Facebook can gather intimate biometric data when we show them our face” (The Important Risks And Dangers Of Virtual And Augmented Reality)
